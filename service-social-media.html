Common Questions

Social Media Marketing FAQs

Answers to questions local business owners ask most.

It depends on your audience and business type. For most local businesses, Facebook remains essential for local reach and community groups. Instagram works well for visual businesses (restaurants, salons, retail). TikTok is growing for discovery and reaching younger audiences. LinkedIn is valuable for B2B services. We'll analyze your specific situation and recommend the platforms where your customers actually are.
Quality beats quantity, but consistency matters. For most local businesses, we recommend 3-5 feed posts per week, daily stories, and 2-3 reels per week. The key is maintaining a consistent presence so the algorithm favors your content. We handle all the scheduling so you don't have to worry about it.
Organic social media is a long-term strategy—expect to see meaningful engagement improvements within 2-3 months and significant follower growth within 6 months. Paid social advertising can generate leads immediately. The combination of consistent organic content plus strategic paid campaigns creates compounding results over time.
Behind-the-scenes content, team introductions, customer stories, before/after reveals, local community involvement, and educational tips consistently perform well. Video content—especially short-form reels—gets significantly more reach than static images. Authenticity wins: customers want to see the real people behind the business, not polished corporate content.
Organic reach on platforms like Facebook has declined significantly over the years. While organic content builds brand and community, paid advertising is typically needed to reach new customers at scale. We recommend a hybrid approach: consistent organic content to nurture your audience, plus targeted paid campaigns to expand reach and drive specific actions like website visits or lead generation.
